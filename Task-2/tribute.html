Tribute to Savitri

1917 - 1981

Savitri was one of the most talented and popular actresses of Indian cinema, known for her mesmerizing performances and her natural beauty. She was born in Chirravuru, Andhra Pradesh, India, in 1917, and started her acting career in the 1940s.

During her long and illustrious career, Savitri starred in more than 300 films in various languages, including Telugu, Tamil, Kannada, and Hindi. She was known for her versatile acting skills and her ability to bring complex characters to life on the screen.

Some of her most memorable roles include the tragic heroine in the film Devadasu (1953), the feisty village girl in the film Maya Bazaar (1957), and the strong-willed woman in the film Gundamma Katha (1962). She also produced and directed some of her own films, and was a trailblazer for women in Indian cinema.

Savitri passed away in 1981, but her legacy lives on through her timeless performances and her impact on Indian cinema. She will always be remembered as one of the greatest actresses of all time.
